PILA

Ukendt Dansk computerspil perle, pludselig fundet og udgivet efter at have ligget i gemmerne i 20 år.

Indehaveren af retro spil, legetøj og frisør butikken "Ruben og Bobby" på Nørrebro, Bobby Ågren blev noget overrasket, da han pludselig blev præsenteret for et platformsspil som de to venner Kasper Kabell Kristensen og Jon Auring Grimm lavede for 20 år siden. Spillet "Pila" var aldrig blevet udgivet, fordi de to venner var bange for, at andre ikke ville kunne se spillets genialiteter. Bobby var fra start af meget begejstret for spillet: "Pila ligger fuldt på højde med de andre store danskespilklassikere fra 80-90erne som "Kaptajn Kaper i Kattegat, Battle Squadron og Jungledyret Hugo. Og er bedre end "Super Oswald, Harald Hårdtand, Skærmtrolden Hugo og Guldkornsexpressen"

"At et dansk spil af den her kaliber pludselig dukker op 20 år senere svarer til, at der blev fundet et vhs bånd fra 1997 med en dansk dogme film ingen kendte til" Spillet har verdenspremiere på Scandinaviens største retro spil messe "Retro Spel Mässan" i Gøteborg" og udkommer digitalt d. 29 April 2017. Spillet kan købes fysisk i Ruben og Bobby Nørrebro afdeling eller hentes digitalt her: https://remedium.itch.io/pila

Så så mange som muligt kan komme til at stifte bekendskab med dette fantastiske spil. English: A puzzle/action arcade extravaganza!

Pila is a top-down view puzzle/action game with an abundance of diverse gameplay elements and great level designs. You - a terribly cute and innocent alien - must steer your high-tech Pila™ ball suit through labyrinthic courses in an elimination race with other aliens for the grand prize: a lifelong place in the Emperor's sulfur mines. Smooth physics and precise controls make for an amazing gameplay, that you will learn to master as you progress through the many levels. It starts out nice and easy, but ends in an inferno of fast paced action with the final Hardcore Labyrinths' bullet hell style challenges being likely the hardest arcade gaming you've ever faced! Background Pila is a jewel from the golden age of home arcade gaming. It was created by childhood friends Kasper Kabell and Jon Auring Grimm in 1996-99 during their high school years. The two had collaborated for years on game development and animations on the Amiga and PC before starting the work on Pila. In 2017 Pila was discovered and brought to glory by renowned retro gaming guru Bobby, who owns the combined retro gaming and toy store, barber shop and arcade in Copenhagen, Ruben og Bobby. Pila was developed for MS-DOS in Turbo Pascal and Assembly Language using no game development tools. All graphics were done in amazing Deluxe Paint. Thanks to DOSBox (the open source DOS emulator), the game now runs smoothly on PC and Mac.
